## 💰 US Tax Terms & Employment Models (Detailed)

### 1. W2 (Hourly or Salary)
The most common employment type for US Citizens and Green Card holders.
* **The Model:** The candidate is a direct employee of your agency or the client.
* **Tax Responsibility:** The employer (you/your client) is responsible for withholding **Federal Income Tax**, **State Income Tax**, and the employee's share of **FICA** (Social Security & Medicare).
* **Employer Burden:** You must pay an additional ~10-15% on top of the pay rate for "Employer-side" taxes (FICA match, FUTA, SUI) and Workers' Comp insurance.
* **Benefits:** Usually includes health insurance, 401k, and Paid Time Off (PTO).

### 2. C2C (Corp-to-Corp)
The standard for H1B and other visa holders working through vendors.
* **The Model:** Your company signs a contract with the candidate's own company (usually an LLC or S-Corp). 
* **Tax Responsibility:** The candidate's company is 100% responsible for all taxes. You pay the "Gross" hourly rate.
* **Compliance:** Requires a Certificate of Insurance (COI), Federal Employer Identification Number (FEIN), and an Articles of Incorporation document.
* **Best for:** High-rate consultants and visa-dependent candidates.

### 3. 1099 (Independent Contractor)
Used for self-employed individuals (mostly US Citizens/GC).
* **The Model:** The individual is treated as a business. No taxes are withheld from their check.
* **Tax Responsibility:** The candidate pays "Self-Employment Tax" (both the employer and employee share of FICA).
* **Note:** Use this sparingly for IT roles; the IRS has strict rules about who qualifies as a 1099 vs. a W2 employee.

## 🏛️ Comprehensive US Tax & Employment Deep-Dive

### 1. W2 (The "Full-Service" Model)
This is the standard for most US-based hires. 
* **Detailed Explanation:** Under a W2, the employer acts as a tax agent for the government. You must withhold a percentage of the candidate's pay for Federal and State taxes.
* **The "Double Tax" (FICA):** FICA consists of Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%). On W2, the **Employer** pays 7.65% and the **Employee** pays 7.65%.
* **Why Candidates Like It:** They get "benefits" (401k, Health) and don't have to worry about filing complex business taxes.
* **Why Recruiters Care:** Your "Margin" is lower because of the ~15% payroll burden you must pay on top of their salary.

### 2. C2C (The "Business-to-Business" Model)
Commonly used for H1B, L1, and EAD candidates working through a vendor or their own company.
* **Detailed Explanation:** You are not hiring a "person"; you are hiring a "Company." No taxes are withheld by you.
* **Tax Compliance:** The candidate's company (the "Vendor") is responsible for all payroll taxes.
* **Key Document:** **Form W-9** is used to collect the company's Tax ID (EIN).
* **Why Recruiters Care:** It’s "cleaner" for your accounting—the rate you agree on ($70/hr) is exactly what your company pays. No hidden burden.

### 3. 1099 (The "Independent" Model)
* **Detailed Explanation:** Used for independent contractors who are not incorporated. 
* **Self-Employment Tax:** Since there is no "employer" to match the 7.65% FICA, the candidate pays the full **15.3%** themselves.
* **IRS Form:** At the end of the year, you send them a **Form 1099-NEC** showing how much you paid them.

### 🇨🇦 Canada (The Staffing Perspective)
Canada uses a "Point System" (Express Entry) and is generally more flexible than the US for international talent.

* **1. PGWP (Post-Graduation Work Permit):** * The Canadian version of OPT. Issued to students after graduation. It is an **Open Work Permit**, meaning they can work for any recruiter/client.
* **2. Closed Work Permit (LMIA):** * **LMIA (Labour Market Impact Assessment):** The employer must prove no Canadian was available. These candidates are "tied" to one employer and cannot be easily placed elsewhere by a recruiter.
* **3. Open Work Permit:** * Often given to spouses of skilled workers or those awaiting Permanent Residency (PR). These are high-value candidates for staffing agencies because they don't need sponsorship.
* **4. GTS (Global Talent Stream):** * A fast-track 2-week processing visa for high-demand IT roles (Developers, Data Scientists). Great for "Headhunting" talent from outside Canada.

## 🚩 Recruiter Red Flags (Visa Screening)
1. **Validity:** Always ask, *"When does your current work authorization expire?"*
2. **Cap Gap:** For F1-OPT students, check if they are in the "Cap Gap" period waiting for an H1B lottery result.
3. **Passport:** For TN Visas (US), ensure the candidate holds a **Canadian/Mexican Passport**, not just PR status.
4. **C2C Compliance:** For H1B candidates on C2C, always ask for the **Copy of the I-797 Approval Notice** to verify the employer.
